What is Bharatanatyam?

Bharatanatyam is one of the oldest classical dance forms of India, originating from Tamil Nadu. Known for its intricate footwork, sculpturesque poses, and expressive storytelling, Bharatanatyam captures the essence of devotion and spirituality.

This dance form is deeply rooted in temple traditions and is often used as a medium to depict stories of gods and goddesses.

The Rich History of Bharatanatyam

Bharatanatyam has its roots in the Natya Shastra, an ancient Sanskrit text on performing arts. Originally performed in temples as an offering to the deities, Bharatanatyam was a form of devotion and storytelling. Over time, it transitioned from temples to the stage, retaining its spiritual essence while evolving into a celebrated classical art form worldwide.
